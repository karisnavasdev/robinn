What VEX is

Not a chatbot. A desktop runtime that runs missions against real markets, under rules the model cannot override.

Article I Enforce

Nothing signs outside your limits.

Seven checks run before every trade: killswitch, blocked coins, daily loss, losing streak, open positions, trade count, size cap. The first fail blocks the trade, and the block itself is recorded.

Article II Prove

Every action lands on a numbered record.

Wins, losses, and blocked attempts become one anchored audit log anyone can verify. No screenshots, no cherry-picked highlight reel, and your strategy stays private.

Article III Local

Your keys never leave your machine.

Runtime, memory, audit log, and custody all live on your hardware. No cloud relay, no custodial server. The code is open on GitHub.

Answers

The questions that decide trust.

01 Can VEX move funds without me?

No. The model can only propose. Every state-changing action is prepared as an intent, checked against your policy, and executed only by the runtime. In approval mode, nothing signs until you say yes.

02 Where do my private keys live?

On your machine, never transmitted to VEX. Keys are held by the local runtime and used only at the signing boundary, after an action clears the policy gate. No cloud relay, no custodial server, no remote key escrow.

03 Is any of this cloud-dependent?

No. VEX is a native desktop app. Runtime, memory, audit logs, and custody all live locally. The only outbound calls are the protocol and model endpoints you explicitly enable; your strategy and keys are never sent to us.
